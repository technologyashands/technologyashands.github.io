##MY USE OF TECHNOLOGY:
"A photograph captures a real scene in front of me, and after clicking
my camera the file is saved to memory to be downloaded, uploaded or
printed out. In light of a proliferation of images in our culture and
the popularization of photography through social media platforms, I
create alternative mechanisms for photographs to be displayed beyond
prints on paper or jpegs on a screen. How photographs are viewed and
experienced is the focus of my work and my practice integrates images
within objects, both found and hand made, tangible and experiential.
Placed within objects and architectural spaces, my photographs are
displayed in unique settings that force the viewer to interact with
them in non-traditional ways: within curtains you can walk through,
placed behind windows to present faux-views, and inside boxes that
require the viewer to contort their bodies to view them properly.
These actions allow my viewer to confront my images with a similar
effort and experience that I had when looking through my viewfinder,
pointing and deciding to shoot." - Ryan Frank
